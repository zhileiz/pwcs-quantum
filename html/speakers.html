Ying Shuling is the founder and CEO of Hero Entertainment Group (ticker symbol: 430127),Executive Vice President of the E-sports Gaming Sub-committee of Chinese Ministry of Culture, and Chairman of the Hong Kong Electronic Sports Federation. He is a pioneer in China’s mobile game launching industry, and a forerunner of the mobile e-sports gaming. He is the captain of the Hero Racing fleet that has won the grand champion of China Cup International Regatta in 2014, 2015 and 2016.

On June 16, 2015, Ying Shuling founded Hero Entertainment – China’s “first share in mobile e-sports” which was successfully listed in the country. H.BROTHERS, Sequoia Capital, Focus Media, Zhen Fund, China Renaissance Partners and Wanzhong Tiandi made a joint investment of in Hero Entertainment. Hero Entertainment is geared to the strategic goal of pan-entertainment, promotion of “mobile e-sports gaming” and development of the Internet sports. The company is committed to providing users at home and abroad with high-caliber mobile gaming content . The company is the largest Chinese cultural exporting company in Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan and Southeast Asia.

Sai Wang
Founder of Yiqiao
×

Steven Wang is the founder and chairperson of Yiqiao, a social initiative and platform that cultivates top graduates and young professionals to serve in the social sector in China. The organized is supported by Gates Foundation and a number of leading Chinese foundations. Steven completed a Master of Public Policy degree at Oxford University on the Rhodes Scholarship. He was part of the founding staff of the Yenching Academy at Peking University and served as a Program Manager at Teach for China based in Yunnan. Steven has studied international relations in Toronto, Paris, and Jerusalem and is on an extended deferral from Harvard Law School. He has also worked on development projects in East Africa and legal aid programs for migrant workers in the suburbs of Beijing. Steven is the ambassador for the Rhodes Scholarship in China and a World Economic Forum Global Shaper. He serves as a mentor and advisor to the Schwarzman scholars at Tsinghua University. Steven was selected this year for Forbes 30 Under 30 in China, representing a new generation of social leaders bridging China and the world.

Yiqiao (益桥) is a social initiative and platform that recruits, places, and cultivates top young Chinese graduates and professionals to serve in the social sector through a prestigious service fellowship. The fellowship provides stipend, intensive training, mentorship and coaching, and continuous community support. The first cohort of fellows were chosen from applications from around the world, and the final ten were announced at the Bird’s Nest in Beijing. Fellows are currently at work in nonprofits, foundations, and social enterprises across China, tackling issues of education, migrant workers, poverty, environment, etc. They come from a range of backgrounds, including consulting, academia, international organizations, and the countryside. Yiqiao aspires to cultivate a new generation of young Chinese with a sense of social responsibility and global citizenship who can be a bridge between the privileged and underprivileged, the private and social sector, China and the world. Its work has been reported by major national news outlets such as Xinhua and Forbes.
